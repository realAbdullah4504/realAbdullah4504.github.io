
Very clean.

Just immediate clarity.

## Home Page Structure

Instead of randomly adding sections, we'll answer one question:

**What should a visitor learn as they scroll?**

Suggested sequence:

1. Hero
2. Engineering Overview
3. Featured Projects
4. Engineering Capabilities
5. Professional Experience
6. Technical Skills
7. Call To Action

## Sections

### 1. Hero

Purpose: Who are you?

### 2. Engineering Overview

This is not an "About Me."

Instead, it answers: *What kind of engineer are you?*

Examples:

- Full-stack development
- Cloud infrastructure
- DevOps
- Distributed systems
- Production architecture

This tells visitors what problems you solve.

### 3. Featured Projects

This is where you earn credibility.

Instead of six equal cards, feature your strongest work first:

- Tender Procurement Intelligence Platform
- Event-Driven AWS Platform
- Tax Rebate Platform

The rest can appear below or on the Projects page.

### 4. Engineering Capabilities

This is one of your unique strengths because you've already organized your capabilities.

Rather than listing tools, group them by what you can accomplish:

- Backend Engineering
- Cloud Infrastructure
- DevOps & CI/CD
- Distributed Systems
- Frontend Development
- Databases & Messaging
- Observability

This communicates capabilities, not just technologies.

### 5. Professional Experience

A concise timeline of your roles, with links to more detail if needed.

### 6. Technical Skills

Here you can present the technologies in a clean, organized way.

### 7. Call to Action

Finish with a simple invitation:

- Download Resume
- View GitHub
- Connect on LinkedIn
- Contact Me
