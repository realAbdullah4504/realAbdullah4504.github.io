# About

I'm a backend systems engineer focused on building software that solves operational problems through scalable architecture, automation, and well-structured workflows.

My work centers on designing systems that continue to perform as products grow in complexity. Rather than focusing on isolated features, I approach projects as complete systems—understanding the business process, defining clear workflows, designing maintainable architectures, and implementing reliable solutions.

My primary areas of expertise include:

* Backend system architecture
* Workflow automation
* Distributed processing systems
* Cloud infrastructure and DevOps
* Data extraction and processing pipelines
* AI-enhanced backend workflows

Many of the systems I've built involve long-running processes, state-driven workflows, distributed workers, automation pipelines, and integrations across multiple services. Whether the goal is processing procurement data, automating tax workflows, managing marketplace operations, or deploying applications through CI/CD pipelines, I focus on building systems that are maintainable, observable, and designed for production use.

## My Engineering Approach

Every project begins with understanding the business problem before selecting technologies.

I generally work through the following process:

1. Understand the operational workflow and business objectives.
2. Design the system architecture and define service boundaries.
3. Model workflows and state transitions where appropriate.
4. Build modular, maintainable backend components.
5. Automate repetitive processes and reduce manual operations.
6. Deploy using reliable infrastructure and CI/CD practices.
7. Monitor, iterate, and improve based on real-world usage.

I value simplicity in architecture. My goal is to design systems that remain understandable, extensible, and reliable over time.
